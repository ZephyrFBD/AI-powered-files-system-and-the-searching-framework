"I'd rather not. I want to go to the studio."
I hesitated a moment.
"Would you like me to come with you?" I asked then.
"No; I should prefer to be alone."
"All right."
I gave the driver the necessary direction, and in renewed silence we drove on. Dirk had not been to 
the studio since the wretched morning on which they had taken Blanche to the hospital. I was glad 
he did not want me to accompany him, and when I left him at the door I walked away with relief. I 
took a new pleasure in the streets of Paris, and I looked with smiling eyes at the people who 
hurried to and fro. The day was fine and sunny, and I felt in myself a more acute delight in life. I
