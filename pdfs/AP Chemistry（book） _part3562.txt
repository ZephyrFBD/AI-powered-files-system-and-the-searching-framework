Phosphorus chlorides, bromides, and iodides can be made by direct oxi-
dation of elemental phosphorus with the elemental halogen. PCl3, for exam-
ple, which is a liquid at room temperature, is made by passing a stream of dry 
chlorine gas over white or red phosphorus:
 
2 P1s2 + 3 Cl21g2 ¡ 2 PCl31l2 
[22.44]
If excess chlorine gas is present, an equilibrium is established between PCl3 and 
PCl5.
 
PCl31l2 + Cl21g2 ∆ PCl51s2
