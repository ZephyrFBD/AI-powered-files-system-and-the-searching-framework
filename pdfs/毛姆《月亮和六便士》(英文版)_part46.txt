She smiled, and her eyes shone with a malice I knew already. It meant that she had heard some 
scandal about one of her friends, and the instinct of the literary woman was all alert.
"You did meet Charles Strickland, didn't you?"
Not only her face, but her whole body, gave a sense of alacrity. I nodded. I wondered if the poor 
devil had been hammered on the Stock Exchange or run over by an omnibus.
"Isn't it dreadful? He's run away from his wife."
Miss Waterford certainly felt that she could not do her subject justice on the curb of Jermyn Street, 
and so, like an artist, flung the bare fact at me and declared that she knew no details. I could not do 
her the injustice of supposing that so trifling a circumstance would have prevented her from giving 
them, but she was obstinate.
