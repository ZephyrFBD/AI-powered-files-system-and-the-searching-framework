Animals are complex systems of cells working in a coordinated fashion to monitor changing external conditions
while maintaining a constant internal environment. To accomplish these tasks, animal cells are organized into
systems that are specialized for particular functions. This chapter focuses on the structure of these various systems
and how they accomplish particular tasks.
