INTRODUCTION
axonomy or classification is the naming and classification of
species. It began in the eighteenth century when Carolus Linnaeus
developed the system used today, the system of binomial nomenclature.
This system has two main characteristics: a two-part name for every
organism (for example, human is Homo sapiens and lion is Panthera leo),
and a hierarchical classification of species into broader groups of
organisms. These broader groups, or taxa, in order from the general to the
specific are kingdom, phylum, class, order, family, genus, and species.
