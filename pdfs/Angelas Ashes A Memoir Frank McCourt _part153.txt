 I did not like the jackdaws that perched on trees and gravestones 
 
 and I did not want to leave Oliver with them. I threw a rock at a jackdaw 
 
 that waddled over toward Oliver’s grave. Dad said I shouldn’t 
 
 throw rocks at jackdaws, they might be somebody’s soul. I didn’t know 
 
 what a soul was but I didn’t ask him because I didn’t care. Oliver was 
 
 dead and I hated jackdaws. I’d be a man someday and I’d come back 
 
 with a bag of rocks and I’d leave the graveyard littered with dead 
 
 jackdaws. 
 
 The morning after Oliver’s burial Dad went to the Labour Exchange to

 he’d be home by noon, that he’d get coal and make a fire, that we’d have
