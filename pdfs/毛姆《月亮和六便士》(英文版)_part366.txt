We were all silent for a while, and Tiare fished out of her capacious pocket a handful of cigarettes. 
She handed one to each of us, and we all three smoked. At last she said:
"Since ce monsieur is interested in Strickland, why do you not take him to see Dr. Coutras? He can 
tell him something about his illness and death."
"Volontiers," said the Captain, looking at me.
I thanked him, and he looked at his watch.
"It is past six o'clock. We should find him at home if you care to come now."
I got up without further ado, and we walked along the road that led to the doctor's house. He lived 
out of the town, but the Hotel de la Fleur was on the edge of it, and we were quickly in the country.

vanilla. The pirate birds were screeching among the leaves of the palms. We came to a stone
