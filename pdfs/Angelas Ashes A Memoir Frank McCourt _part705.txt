 At the end of the week Mrs. O’Connell hands me the first wages of my 
 
 life, a pound, my first pound. I run down the stairs and up to O’Connell 
 
 Street, the main street, where the lights are on and people are going 
 
 home from work, people like me with wages in their pockets. I want 
 
 them to know I’m like them, I’m a man, I have a pound. I walk up one 
 
 side of O’Connell Street and down the other and hope they’ll notice 
 
 me.They don’t. I want to wave my pound note at the world so they’ll 
 
 say,There he goes, Frankie McCourt the workingman, with a pound in 
 
 his pocket.
