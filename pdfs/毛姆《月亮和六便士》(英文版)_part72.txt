The door was at the side of a shop. It stood open, and just within was a sign: Bureau au premier. I 
walked up narrow stairs, and on the landing found a sort of box, glassed in, within which were a 
desk and a couple of chairs. There was a bench outside, on which it might be presumed the night 
porter passed uneasy nights. There was no one about, but under an electric bell was written 
Garcon. I rang, and presently a waiter appeared. He was a young man with furtive eyes and a 
sullen look. He was in shirt-sleeves and carpet slippers.
I do not know why I made my enquiry as casual as possible.
"Does Mr. Strickland live here by any chance?" I asked.
"Number thirty-two. On the sixth floor."
I was so surprised that for a moment I did not answer.
"Is he in?"
The waiter looked at a board in the bureau.
