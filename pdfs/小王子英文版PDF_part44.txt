"Millions of what?"
The businessman suddenly realized that there was no hope of being left in peace
until he answered this question.
"Millions of those little objects," he said, "which one sometimes sees in the sky."
"Flies?"
"Oh, no. Little glittering objects."
"Bees?"
"Oh, no. Little golden objects that set lazy men to idle dreaming. As for me, I am
concerned with mattersof consequence. There is no time for idle dreaming in my life."
"Ah! You mean the stars?"
"Yes, that‘s it. The stars."
"And what do you do with five­hundred millions of stars?"
"Five­hundred­and­one million, six­hundred­twenty­two thousand, seven­hundred­
thirty­one. I am concerned with matters of consequence: I am accurate."
