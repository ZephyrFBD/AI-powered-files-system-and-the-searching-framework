"Then he said: `But what does Ata say to it?' `It appears that she has a beguin for you,' I said. 
`She's willing if you are. Shall I call her?' He chuckled in a funny, dry way he had, and I called her. 
She knew what I was talking about, the hussy, and I saw her out of the corner of my eyes listening 
with all her ears, while she pretended to iron a blouse that she had been washing for me. She came. 
She was laughing, but I could see that she was a little shy, and Strickland looked at her without 
speaking."
