"I'd been scrubbing the deck, and all at once a chap said to me: `Why, there it is.' And I looked up 
and I saw the outline of the island. I knew right away that there was the place I'd been looking for 
all my life. Then we came near, and I seemed to recognise it. Sometimes when I walk about it all 
seems familiar. I could swear I've lived here before."
"Sometimes it takes them like that," said Tiare. "I've known men come on shore for a few hours 
while their ship was taking in cargo, and never go back. And I've known men who came here to be 
in an office for a year, and they cursed the place, and when they went away they took their dying 
oath they'd hang themselves before they came back again, and in six months you'd see them land 
once more, and they'd tell you they couldn't live anywhere else."
