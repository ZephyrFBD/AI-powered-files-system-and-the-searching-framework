were penniless and the night was bitter cold, they were glad to borrow from anyone who had 
earned a stray franc during the day the price of a roof over their heads. They were not niggardly, 
these tramps, and he who had money did not hesitate to share it among the rest. They belonged to 
all the countries in the world, but this was no bar to good-fellowship; for they felt themselves 
freemen of a country whose frontiers include them all, the great country of Cockaine.
