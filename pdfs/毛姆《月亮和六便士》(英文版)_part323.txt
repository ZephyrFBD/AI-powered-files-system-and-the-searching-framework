"I guess you'd better get out of Marseilles before Tough Bill comes out of hospital," he said to 
Strickland, when they had got back to the Chink's Head and were cleaning themselves.
"This beats cock-fighting," said Strickland.
I could see his sardonic smile.
Captain Nichols was anxious. He knew Tough Bill's vindictiveness. Strickland had downed the 
mulatto twice, and the mulatto, sober, was a man to be reckoned with. He would bide his time 
stealthily. He would be in no hurry, but one night Strickland would get a knife-thrust in his back, 
and in a day or two the corpse of a nameless beach-comber would be fished out of the dirty water
