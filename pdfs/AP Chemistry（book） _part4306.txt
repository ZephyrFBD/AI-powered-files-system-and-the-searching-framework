Haber process The catalyst system and conditions of 
temperature and pressure developed by Fritz Haber and 
coworkers for the formation of NH3 from H2 and N2. (Section 15.2)
half-life The time required for the concentration of a reactant 
substance to decrease to half its initial value; the time required 
for half of a sample of a particular radioisotope to decay. 
(Sections 14.4 and 21.4)
half-reaction An equation for either an oxidation or a reduction 
that explicitly shows the electrons involved, for example, 
Zn2 +1aq2 + 2 e- ¡ Zn1s2. (Section 20.2)
halogens Members of group 7A in the periodic table. 
(Section 7.8)
heat The energy that causes the temperature of an object to 
increase. The flow of energy from a body at higher temperature 
to one at lower temperature when they are placed in thermal 
contact. (Sections 1.4 and 1.5)
heat capacity The quantity of heat required to raise the 
temperature of a sample of matter by 1 °C (or 1 K). (Section 5.5)
