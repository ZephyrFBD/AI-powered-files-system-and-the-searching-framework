"Never knew him say a cross word," answered the Captain. "He'd be a bit surly sometimes, but 
when we hadn't had a bite since morning, and we hadn't even got the price of a lie down at the 
Chink's, he'd be as lively as a cricket."
I was not surprised at this. Strickland was just the man to rise superior to circumstances, when they 
were such as to occasion despondency in most; but whether this was due to equanimity of soul or 
to contradictoriness it would be difficult to say.
