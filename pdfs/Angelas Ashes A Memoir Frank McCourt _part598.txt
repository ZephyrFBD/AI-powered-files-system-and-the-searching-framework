 There’s a letter from Dad. He’s coming home two days before Christmas. 
 
 He says everything will be different, he’s a new man, he hopes 
 
 we’re good boys, obeying our mother, attending to our religious duties, 
 
 and he’s bringing us all something for Christmas. 
 
 Mam takes me to the railway station to meet him. The station is 
 
 always exciting with all the coming and going, people leaning from carriages, 
 
 crying, smiling,waving good-bye, the train hooting and calling, 
 
 chugging away in clouds of steam, people sniffling on the platform, the 
 
 railway tracks silvering into the distance, on to Dublin and the world 
 
 beyond.
