Chapter XL
For the next month, occupied with my own affairs, I saw no one connected with this lamentable 
business, and my mind ceased to be occupied with it. But one day, when I was walking along, bent 
on some errand, I passed Charles Strickland. The sight of him brought back to me all the horror 
which I was not unwilling to forget, and I felt in me a sudden repulsion for the cause of it. 
Nodding, for it would have been childish to cut him, I walked on quickly; but in a minute I felt a 
hand on my shoulder.
"You're in a great hurry," he said cordially.
