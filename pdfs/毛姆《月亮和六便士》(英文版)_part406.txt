Mr. Van Busche Taylor was a very thin man with a large, bald head, bony and shining; and under 
the great dome of his skull his face, yellow, with deep lines in it, looked very small. He was quiet 
and exceedingly polite. He spoke with the accent of New England, and there was about his 
demeanour a bloodless frigidity which made me ask myself why on earth he was busying himself 
with Charles Strickland. I had been slightly tickled at the gentleness which Mrs. Strickland put into 
her mention of her husband's name, and while the pair conversed I took stock of the room in which
