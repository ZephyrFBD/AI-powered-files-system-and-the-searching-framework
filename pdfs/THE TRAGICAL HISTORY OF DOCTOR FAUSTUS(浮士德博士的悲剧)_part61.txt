FAUSTUS. These<153> gracious words, most royal Carolus, Shall
make poor Faustus, to his utmost power, Both love and serve the German
Emperor, And lay his life at holy Bruno's feet: For proof whereof, if so
your grace be pleas'd, The doctor stands prepar'd by power of art To cast
his magic charms, that shall pierce through<154> The ebon gates of ever-
burning hell, And hale the stubborn Furies from their caves, To compass
whatsoe'er your grace commands.
