was the very day, and I had received no word from Mrs. Strickland. Did she want to see me or did 
she not? It was likely enough that in the agitation of the moment my note had escaped her memory. 
Perhaps I should be wiser not to go. On the other hand, she might wish to keep the affair quiet, and 
it might be highly indiscreet on my part to give any sign that this strange news had reached me. I 
was torn between the fear of hurting a nice woman's feelings and the fear of being in the way. I felt 
she must be suffering, and I did not want to see a pain which I could not help; but in my heart was 
a desire, that I felt a little ashamed of, to see how she was taking it. I did not know what to do.
