Labor and Birth
Labor is the physical efforts of expulsion of the fetus and the placenta from the uterus during birth (parturition). Toward the
end of the third trimester, estrogen causes receptors on the uterine wall to develop and bind the hormone oxytocin. At this
time, the baby reorients, facing forward and down with the back or crown of the head engaging the cervix (uterine opening).
