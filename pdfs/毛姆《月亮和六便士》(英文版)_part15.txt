season. Heaven knows what pains the author has been at, what bitter experiences he has endured 
and what heartache suffered, to give some chance reader a few hours' relaxation or to while away 
the tedium of a journey. And if I may judge from the reviews, many of these books are well and 
carefully written; much thought has gone to their composition; to some even has been given the 
anxious labour of a lifetime. The moral I draw is that the writer should seek his reward in the 
pleasure of his work and in release from the burden of his thought; and, indifferent to aught else, 
care nothing for praise or censure, failure or success.
