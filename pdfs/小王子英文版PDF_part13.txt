If you were to say to the grown­ups: "I saw a beautiful house made of rosy brick, with
geraniums in the windows and doves on the roof," they would not be able to get any idea
of that house at all. You would have to say to them: "I saw a house that cost $20,000."
Then they would exclaim: "Oh, what a pretty house that is!"
Just so, you might say to them: "The proof that the little prince existed is that he was
charming, that he laughed, and that he was looking for a sheep. If anybody wants a
sheep, that is a proof that he exists." And what good would it do to tell them that? They
would shrug their shoulders, and treat you like a child. But if you said to them: "The
planet he came from is Asteroid B­612," then they would be convinced, and leave you in
peace from their questions.
They are like that. One must not hold it against them. Children should always show
great forbearancetoward grown­up people.
