"She'll get over it."
I cannot describe the extraordinary callousness with which he made this reply. It disconcerted me, 
but I did my best not to show it. I adopted the tone used by my Uncle Henry, a clergyman, when he 
was asking one of his relatives for a subscription to the Additional Curates Society.
"You don't mind my talking to you frankly?"
He shook his head, smiling.
"Has she deserved that you should treat her like this?"
"No."
"Have you any complaint to make against her?"
"None."
"Then, isn't it monstrous to leave her in this fashion, after seventeen years of married life, without 
a fault to find with her?"
"Monstrous."
