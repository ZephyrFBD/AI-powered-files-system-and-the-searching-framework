I think Mrs. Strickland was glad to show me her children, and she accepted my invitation with 
alacrity. They were even more attractive than their photographs had suggested, and she was right 
to be proud of them. I was young enough for them not to feel shy, and they chattered merrily about 
one thing and another. They were extraordinarily nice, healthy young children. It was very 
agreeable under the trees.
When in an hour they crowded into a cab to go home, I strolled idly to my club. I was perhaps a 
little lonely, and it was with a touch of envy that I thought of the pleasant family life of which I 
had had a glimpse. They seemed devoted to one another. They had little private jokes of their own 
which, unintelligible to the outsider, amused them enormously. Perhaps Charles Strickland was
