I let him take me to a restaurant of his choice, but on the way I bought a paper. When we had 
ordered our dinner, I propped it against a bottle of St. Galmier and began to read. We ate in 
silence. I felt him looking at me now and again, but I took no notice. I meant to force him to 
conversation.
"Is there anything in the paper?" he said, as we approached the end of our silent meal.
I fancied there was in his tone a slight note of exasperation.
"I always like to read the feuilleton on the drama," I said.
I folded the paper and put it down beside me.
"I've enjoyed my dinner," he remarked.
"I think we might have our coffee here, don't you?"
"Yes."
We lit our cigars. I smoked in silence. I noticed that now and then his eyes rested on me with a 
faint smile of amusement. I waited patiently.
"What have you been up to since I saw you last?" he asked at length.
