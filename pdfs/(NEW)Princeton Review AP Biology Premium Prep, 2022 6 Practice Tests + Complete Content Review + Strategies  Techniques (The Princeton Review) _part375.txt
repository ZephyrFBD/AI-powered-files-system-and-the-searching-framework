Chapter 10
Natural Selection
All of the organisms we see today arose from earlier organisms. This process, known as evolution, can be described
as a change in a population over time. Interestingly, however, the driving force of evolution, called natural
selection, operates on the level of the individual. In other words, evolution is defined in terms of populations but
occurs in terms of individuals.
Natural selection acts on individuals, but evolution acts on populations.
