ALL. O, God forbid!
FAUSTUS. God forbade it, indeed; but Faustus hath done it:  for the
vain pleasure of four-and-twenty years hath Faustus lost eternal joy and
felicity.  I writ them a bill with mine own blood: the date is expired; this
is the time, and he will fetch me.
FIRST SCHOLAR. Why did not Faustus tell us of this before, that
divines might have prayed for thee?
FAUSTUS. Oft have I thought to have done so; but the devil
threatened to tear me in pieces, if I named God, to fetch me body and soul,
if I once gave ear to divinity:  and now 'tis<250> too late.  Gentlemen,
away, lest you perish with me.
SECOND SCHOLAR. O, what may we do to save Faustus?
