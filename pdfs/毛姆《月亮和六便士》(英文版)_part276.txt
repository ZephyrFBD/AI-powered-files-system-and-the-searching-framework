When I imagined that on seeing his pictures I should get a clue to the understanding of his strange 
character I was mistaken. They merely increased the astonishment with which he filled me. I was 
more at sea than ever. The only thing that seemed clear to me -- and perhaps even this was fanciful 
-- was that he was passionately striving for liberation from some power that held him. But what the 
power was and what line the liberation would take remained obscure. Each one of us is alone in the 
world. He is shut in a tower of brass, and can communicate with his fellows only by signs, and the
