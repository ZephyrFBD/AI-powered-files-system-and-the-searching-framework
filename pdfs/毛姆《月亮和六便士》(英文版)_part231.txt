day or two; and when, late one evening, Stroeve came to see me I knew it was to tell me she was 
dead. He was absolutely exhausted. His volubility had left him at last, and he sank down wearily 
on my sofa. I felt that no words of condolence availed, and I let him lie there quietly. I feared he 
would think it heartless if I read, so I sat by the window, smoking a pipe, till he felt inclined to 
speak.
"You've been very kind to me," he said at last. "Everyone's been very kind."
"Nonsense," I said, a little embarrassed.
