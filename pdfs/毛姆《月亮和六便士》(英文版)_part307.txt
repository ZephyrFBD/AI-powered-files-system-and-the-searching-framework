I have tried to put some connection into the various things Captain Nichols told me about 
Strickland, and I here set them down in the best order I can. They made one another's acquaintance 
during the latter part of the winter following my last meeting with Strickland in Paris. How he had 
passed the intervening months I do not know, but life must have been very hard, for Captain 
Nichols saw him first in the Asile de Nuit. There was a strike at Marseilles at the time, and 
Strickland, having come to the end of his resources, had apparently found it impossible to earn the 
small sum he needed to keep body and soul together.
