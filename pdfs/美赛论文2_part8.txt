I. 
Introduction 
The recent Ebola Virus Disease (EVD), which broke out in West Africa from 
February 2014, is a sever crisis due to the high mortality rate. Additionally, this 
disease spreads quickly because the poor medical condition in West Africa and much 
more frequent international exchange. Up to 1 February 2015, a total of 22,495 have 
been reported confirmed, probable, and suspected cases of EVD in Guinea, Liberia 
and Sierra Leone, with 8,981 reported deaths according to the World Health 
Organization (WHO). As a result, EVD causes widespread concern in the 
international community.
