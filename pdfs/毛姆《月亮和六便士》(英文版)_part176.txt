Stroeve was sublime.
But it was Blanche who most surprised me. She proved herself not only a capable, but a devoted 
nurse. There was nothing in her to remind you that she had so vehemently struggled against her 
husband's wish to bring Strickland to the studio. She insisted on doing her share of the offices 
needful to the sick. She arranged his bed so that it was possible to change the sheet without 
disturbing him. She washed him. When I remarked on her competence, she told me with that 
pleasant little smile of hers that for a while she had worked in a hospital. She gave no sign that she 
hated Strickland so desperately. She did not speak to him much, but she was quick to forestall his 
wants. For a fortnight it was necessary that someone should stay with him all night, and she took
