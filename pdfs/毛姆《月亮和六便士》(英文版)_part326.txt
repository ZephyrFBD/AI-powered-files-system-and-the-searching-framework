give it up; I started from the beginning in the usual way, and made up my mind I could only tell 
what I knew of Strickland's life in the order in which I learnt the facts.
Those that I have now are fragmentary. I am in the position of a biologist who from a single bone 
must reconstruct not only the appearance of an extinct animal, but its habits. Strickland made no 
particular impression on the people who came in contact with him in Tahiti. To them he was no
