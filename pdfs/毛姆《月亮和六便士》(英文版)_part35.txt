Chapter VI
But when at last I met Charles Strickland, it was under circumstances which allowed me to do no 
more than just make his acquaintance. One morning Mrs. Strickland sent me round a note to say 
that she was giving a dinner-party that evening, and one of her guests had failed her. She asked me 
to stop the gap. She wrote:
"It's only decent to warn you that you will be bored to extinction. It was a thoroughly dull party 
from the beginning, but if you will come I shall be uncommonly grateful. And you and I can have 
a little chat by ourselves."
It was only neighbourly to accept.
