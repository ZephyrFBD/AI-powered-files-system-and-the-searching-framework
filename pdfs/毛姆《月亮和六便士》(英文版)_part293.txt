"He's all right," said Strickland. "I bet he found it hell to paint."
When later, in Vienna, I saw several of Peter Brueghel's pictures, I thought I understood why he 
had attracted Strickland's attention. Here, too, was a man with a vision of the world peculiar to 
himself. I made somewhat copious notes at the time, intending to write something about him, but I 
have lost them, and have now only the recollection of an emotion. He seemed to see his fellow-
creatures grotesquely, and he was angry with them because they were grotesque; life was a
