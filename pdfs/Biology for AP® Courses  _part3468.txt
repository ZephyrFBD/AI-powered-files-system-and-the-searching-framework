Connection for AP® Courses
The information in this section is not within the scope for AP® and does not align with the curriculum framework. However,
as a student of biology it is important to understand the types of bones and bone tissues as well as their functions because
this basic part of anatomy is an excellent example of form follows function. For example, spongy bone tissue is found in
the inner layer of bones and contains blood vessels to deliver nutrients and remove waster from bone cells.
