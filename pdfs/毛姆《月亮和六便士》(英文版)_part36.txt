Strickland gave the polite little laugh with which people acknowledge a facetiousness in which 
they see nothing funny, but did not speak. New arrivals claimed my host's attention, and I was left 
to myself. When at last we were all assembled, waiting for dinner to be announced, I reflected, 
while I chatted with the woman I had been asked to "take in," that civilised man practises a strange 
ingenuity in wasting on tedious exercises the brief span of his life. It was the kind of party which 
makes you wonder why the hostess has troubled to bid her guests, and why the guests have 
troubled to come. There were ten people. They met with indifference, and would part with relief. It
