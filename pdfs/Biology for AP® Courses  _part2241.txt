Understanding phylogeny extends far beyond understanding the evolutionary history of species on Earth. For botanists,
phylogeny acts as a guide to discovering new plants that can be used to make food, medicine, and clothing. For doctors,
phylogenies provide information about the origin of diseases and how to treat them, for example, HIV/AIDS.
