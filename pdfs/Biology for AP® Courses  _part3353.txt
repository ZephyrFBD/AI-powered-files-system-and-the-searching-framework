Hormonal Regulation of Stress
When a threat or danger is perceived, the body responds by releasing hormones that will ready it for the “fight-or-flight”
response. The effects of this response are familiar to anyone who has been in a stressful situation: increased heart rate, dry
mouth, and hair standing up.
