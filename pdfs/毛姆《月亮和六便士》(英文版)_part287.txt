But my study of Strickland's character suffers from a greater defect than my ignorance of many 
facts. Because they were obvious and striking, I have written of his relations to women; and yet 
they were but an insignificant part of his life. It is an irony that they should so tragically have 
affected others. His real life consisted of dreams and of tremendously hard work.
